Renter Attorneys Advocates for Tenants' Entitlements:

Mold

Mold is a type of fungus that grows in multicellular filaments called hyphae. It thrives in warm, damp, and humid environments and can be found on food, walls, and other surfaces. Mold reproduces through spores, which are microscopic and can easily spread through the air.

Sewage Leak

A sewage leak occurs when wastewater from toilets, sinks, and drains escapes from pipes or sewer systems, contaminating the surrounding environment. This can happen due to broken, clogged, or damaged pipes and is a serious health hazard.

Infestations

An infestation occurs when a large number of pests—such as insects, rodents, or other unwanted organisms—invade a space, causing damage and posing health risks. Infestations can happen in homes, buildings, farms, and even the human body (such as lice or parasitic infestations).

Landlord Harassment

Landlord harassment occurs when a landlord intentionally creates difficult or unbearable living conditions to force a tenant to leave or give up their rights. This is illegal in many places and can include actions like cutting off essential services, making threats, or refusing to make necessary repairs.

Carbon Monoxide Poisoning

Carbon monoxide (CO) poisoning happens when you inhale too much carbon monoxide gas, which prevents your blood from carrying oxygen. CO is a colorless, odorless, and tasteless gas, making it extremely dangerous because people often don't realize they're being exposed.

Injuries

An injury is any physical harm or damage to the body caused by an accident, trauma, or medical condition. Injuries can range from minor cuts and bruises to serious, life-threatening conditions like fractures, burns, or head trauma.

Uninhabitability issues

An uninhabitability issue occurs when a rental property is unsafe or unlivable due to severe health hazards, structural problems, or lack of essential services. If a rental unit becomes uninhabitable, tenants may have the legal right to withhold rent, break the lease, or demand repairs.

Improper rent charges

Improper rent charges refer to illegal or unjustified fees that a landlord demands from a tenant. These charges violate rental laws and can include overcharging rent, adding unauthorized fees, or demanding payments that are not in the lease agreement.
